# Results narrative

On the full local reference-backed set of 332 English–Chinese WMT25 records, selectively quantizing Gemma 3's 144 text-MLP gate, up, and down projections to native NF4 obtained 28.30 chrF and 31.37 BLEU. The global q4 organizer baseline obtained 27.67 chrF and 30.80 BLEU. Thus, the layer-aware variant improved chrF by 0.63 and BLEU by 0.56. Paired bootstrap resampling with 10,000 trials found both differences significant (chrF p=0.0003; BLEU p=0.0061). The improvement costs additional capacity: artifact size rises from 7.78 to 10.99 GiB and peak VRAM from 12.04 to 14.64 GiB. The layer-aware system was nevertheless 4.2% faster in local generation (2.175 versus 2.271 seconds per line). Both BitsAndBytes paths were slower than BF16 on the RTX A6000.

The transferred IWSLT q3+Zstd method reached 28.26 chrF and 31.39 BLEU. Its quality was statistically indistinguishable from native selective q4 (chrF p=0.2561; BLEU p=0.3576). Its exact codec payload was 14.46 GiB, but decoded inference required 26.43 GiB peak VRAM—essentially the BF16 baseline's 26.46 GiB. Native selective q4 therefore preserves comparable quality while using a 10.99 GiB artifact and 14.64 GiB peak VRAM. This supports the deployment argument: selected-layer storage coding is scientifically informative but does not preserve its storage reduction in accelerator memory.

Relative to BF16, native selective q4 is 0.15 chrF and 0.28 BLEU lower. The chrF difference is not significant at 0.05 (p=0.0897), while the BLEU difference is (p=0.0339). These local results support the selected-layer hypothesis for English–Chinese, but organizer H100 and WMT26 blind-test evaluation remain necessary.
